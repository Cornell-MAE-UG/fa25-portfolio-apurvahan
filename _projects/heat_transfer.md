I enjoyed doing this assignment because it connected back to MAE 3230: Fluids and it was cool to see where heat transfer and fluids interact. This pset required us to figure out if the flow is laminar or turbulent, get the Reynold's number, and evaluate values using fluid properties. I also learned the difference between average and local heat transfer coefficient and when it is important to use one or the other. I learned about the Nusselt number and why it's useful for problems like this. It was cool to see the differences in how the fluid cools down an object based on the geometry of the object. This is useful because there will be times that I will be working with electronics that require a lot of current and thus are likely to get hot very quickly. Knowing how to find the heat transfer coefficient for different geometries, fluids, and speeds will help me figure out what heat sink geometry is best for my purposes and will cool down my electronics best.
